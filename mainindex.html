Serving Food

The most important things to remember about serving prepared foods are: Keep HOT foods HOT – Keep hot cooked foods between 140°F and 165°F until serving time. Harmful bacteria can grow rapidly below 140°F. When food is cooked to temperatures of 165°F to 212°F, most food-poisoning bacteria is killed.

WHAT IS COOKING?

Hobby

Cooking, cooking, or culinary arts is the art, science, and practice of preparing food for consumption by the use of heat. Cooking techniques and materials vary greatly, ranging from grilling meals over an open fire to utilizing electric stoves to baking in various sorts of ovens, all of which reflect local conditions. Cooking styles are also determined by the cooks' skill levels and training. Cooking is done both at home and by professional cooks and chefs in restaurants and other food facilities. Food preparation using heat or fire is a human-only activity. It may have begun approximately 2 million years ago, while archaeological evidence does not reach back more than 1 million years.
